FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is CareLens a replacement for seeing a doctor?

No. CareLens gives a general risk reading based on what you describe — it's not a medical diagnosis and doesn't replace professional care. If you're in doubt, or symptoms are severe, contact a healthcare provider.

Is my data stored anywhere?

No. Your symptom descriptions and medicine photos are processed to give you a result and are not stored on a server. Your recent-checks history is saved only in your own browser.

Can I use it in a language other than English?

Yes — the symptom checker supports voice input in English, Tamil, Hindi, Telugu, Malayalam, and Kannada. Your speech is translated to English before analysis, and you can review the translation before submitting.

What if I photograph a handwritten prescription?

The medicine scanner currently works best with printed strips, boxes, or labels. Handwritten prescriptions are a much harder problem for OCR, and CareLens will let you know clearly if that's what it's seeing rather than guessing.
